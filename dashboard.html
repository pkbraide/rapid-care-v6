Life-Threatening Emergency

Call 112 for immediate medical, fire, or police assistance

📞 Call 112 Now
When to call 112:
  • Severe bleeding or chest pain
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Stroke symptoms (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
  • Severe injuries or burns
  • Any life-threatening situation

What to Tell the Dispatcher

Have this information ready when calling emergency services

1. Your Location
Exact address and phone number
4. Condition of Patient(s)
Conscious, breathing, bleeding, etc.
2. What Happened
Brief description of emergency
5. Immediate Dangers
Fire, weapons, hazards present
3. Number of Victims
How many people need help
6. Follow Instructions
Stay on the line until told to hang up
